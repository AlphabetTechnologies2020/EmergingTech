    What is Quantum Computing?

    Quantum computing is a field of computing focused on establishing computer technology based on the principles of Quantum Theory proposed by a physicist named Max Planck in 1900. This theory is about the behavior of materials and their energy on atomic and sub-atomic levels.

    The operation of Quantum Computing uses the state of an object to produce a basic unit of quantum information known as Qubit or Quantum Bit based something like the spin of a magnetic field.

    What is Qubit?

    To understand what is a Qubit, Just move back Classical Computing Process. The Computer system that we use in our daily life, based on the Classical computing process, their processing ability is restricted in form of bits that is 0 or 1.While Quantum computing, on the other hand, uses Qubits as we discussed above. This computing process is equipped with the unique ability of subatomic participles that allows them to exist in more than one state i.e. 1 and 0 both at the same time.
    A qubit is a two-level quantum system where the two basis qubit states are usually written as 0 and 1. It can be in 0, 1, or (unlike a Classical Computing bit) in a linear combination of both states.

    Features of Quantum Computers

    Quantum Computers are based on two features of quantum physics:

    1. Entanglement, and
    2. Superposition
    These features add uniqueness, efficiency, and extreme power to quantum computers to handle computer operations at an exponential speed achieved using lesser energy consumption than Classical Computers.
