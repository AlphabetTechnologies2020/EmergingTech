Extended Reality

Extended Reality or XR is one of those technologies that emerge or encompasses all those environments, generated by Computer Graphics(CG) that connect physical and virtual elements with different levels of interactions with and between these elements.

Branches of Extended Reality

Under the shade of XR, there are some more deeply engaging branches of technologies that exist having the ability to take the future of advancements to the next level. These branches of technologies are named as:

  • Augmented Reality (AR),
  • Virtual Reality (VR),
  • Mixed Reality (MR), and
  • Everything in between above all.

    • XR is a parent class which includes the entire spectrum from “the complete physical” to “the complete virtual” environment. Thanks to this evolving innovation and advancements in it, merges the physical and virtual worlds with the continuous development of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI).

    XR can pull you in multi-dimensional directions with endless opportunities to create unique and innovative experiences. To help illustrate this we will explore these technologies as they relate to our five basic senses of sight, hearing, touch, smell, taste, and the potential for a “sixth sense.”

    XR is a rapidly growing field being applied in a wide range of ways, such as the Gaming Industry, Healthcare, Engineering, Entertainment Industry, Real Estate, Retail, Military sector, Training, and Remote Work, around the world are already making the most of technology. Just imagine what it might be like to work and live in our world in the next decade and further in the future.

    Examples of Extended Reality

    Some examples of Extended Reality in various industries are:

  • Health Care:

    • Medical imaging is another area that benefits from XR, and modern MRIs and CT scans make extensive use of this technology. It enables the 3D representation of human bodies instead of the traditional 2D imaging, which improves the efficiency of diagnosing.
    Also, this technology considerably expands the opportunities for surgical training, as students can practice on virtual patients.

  • Entertainment Industry:

    • In the Entertainment Industry, video games are the main consumer of this XR technology. It is no wonder, as the business success of companies of this kind relies heavily on the ability to provide a deeply engaging experience.

  • Real Estate:

    • The Real State may thankful to XR technology, You might be able to shop for a new home anywhere in the world as if you were on-site or head to lunch in some faraway land without having to physically be there. You may also select an architecture and interior design using XR.
